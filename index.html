Projects I Worked On

FELLCHASER

Composer, Sound Designer, Audio Implementation

FELLCHASER is an isometric, potion-slinging action roguelike developed by Let'em CookGames. Initially a senior capstone project at UCI, we worked hard towards a Steam release the following Summer, where it is now available to play for free! This was my biggest project on a team as a solo audio developer, in which I tackled the challenge head-on and pride myself in contributing to the final product. The project extensively used WWise, which I used to implement all of the sound effects and music, even creating adaptive music for in-combat, out-of-combat, and low health game states. What I found equal parts fulfilling and fun was creating creature sounds for the many enemy types and their different attacks.

FELLCHASER is avaible for free on Steam if you would like to support our game!

Project: Ether

Audio Lead, Composer, Sound Designer

Project: Ether is a 3rd-person, narrative-driven stealth game developed by Team: Ether. It was created as a senior capstone project that I was on during my time at UCI. I was in charge of the audio direction for Project: Ether, guiding the audio team in creating a cohesive soundscape that aligned with the game's sonic vision. It was interesting dealing with the duality of organic and inorganic sounds, and deciding when and how to blend the two. The soundtrack and some of the sound effects were created by me, along with the splicing, mixing, and effects for the voice acting. This was my first time handling voice lines and I found the experience smooth and intuitive; it was very satisfying!

Project: Ether is available for free on Steam and Itch.io; feel free to support our game!

Pitaya!

Composer, Sound Designer

Pitaya! is a 3rd-person, cooking-inspired hack-and-slash RPG developed by OnTheRiseGames. It started as a student project for a senior capstone at UCI, but development extended past the submission date for the class. We are aiming for a Steam release in the near future! I was mainly tasked with music composition for Pitaya!, where I tried to match the silly tone of the game by using unconventional sounds and instruments in the music. Examples include but are not limited to the toy piano, train whistles, dinner bells, and squeaky dog toys. I also created creature sounds for Kneadlings (similar to Chao from Sonic) and a stone chef npc.

Keep an eye out for our Steam release soon!

Mech Believe MEGA BRAWL!!

Composer

Mech Believe MEGA BRAWL!! is an isometric, action rougelike where you control a kid's toy mech and battle against other kids' mechs and toys at your school. With an emphasis on boundless imagination, this USC senior thesis project aims to tackle the concept of "play," from a child playing with his toys, to the player playing the game, and the close relationship those two concepts have. On the music side, the blend of Nintendo DS soundfonts and sampled instruments help blur the lines between imagination and the real world. This is all to allow the soundtrack to encapsulate both child-like wonder and blood-pumping action in the soundtrack.

Mech Believe MEGA BRAWL!! is planned to release on Steam soon. Keep an eye out for it!

Hi! I'm Nelson Mencos

I'm a Composer and Sound Designer based in Southern California! I specialize in making music and sound effects for video games. My trek through sound started 10 years ago, where I found myself drawn to electronic music production. Since starting out on FL Studio Mobile all those years ago, I have grown as an artist and learned to meld my two passions—video games and music—into a fulfilling form of art.

In 2024, I graduated from the University of California, Irvine with a B.A. in English and a Minor in Creative Writing. Soon after, I landed a job in Localization QA at NIS America, where I've tested various titles, including: Phantom Brave: the Lost Hero, Disgaea 7 Complete, and Ys X: Proud Nordics.

When I'm not working on personal projects or gaming with my friends, I find myself enjoying other hobbies, such as writing poetry, going to electronic or indie shows, and seeking out coffee at new cafes! When it comes to my favorite game, nothing rivals Pokemon for me.

Feel free to contact me for any audio work, or if you just want to chat!
